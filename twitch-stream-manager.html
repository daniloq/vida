Redesigning the Twitch Dashboard

My Role: Senior Product Designer, Streamer Success Team

"Less interface. More creator success."

Context

The Dashboard is the command center for Twitch creators but it was becoming a cockpit of buttons, tabs, and metrics that too often distracted from the real task: going live and connecting with your audience.

When I joined the project, only 34% of users who showed streaming intent actually streamed within 90 days. Of those, just 3% became part-time or full-time streamers. That‚Äôs not a design problem that‚Äôs a focus problem.

So we asked:
What could we do to help creators focus on what they love doing?

Users

The primary customer is the creator. Specifically, new and small creators that are looking to understand all of the different elements that go into successfully streaming on Twitch.

The secondary customer is existing and established creators that have a grasp on the current complexity of successfully streaming on Twitch but need help understanding and interacting with new features and functionality that Twitch develops.

Design Goals

  1. Show less. Guide more.
    Replace dashboards with decisions.
  2. Let data drive the experience.
    Don‚Äôt make users hunt. Bring actions forward when they matter.
  3. Adapt complexity gradually.
    New creators shouldn‚Äôt see the same controls as pros on day one.

User Testing & Prototype Validation

Before final implementation, we tested an interactive prototype with a mix of new and experienced streamers. Participants were given common streaming tasks to complete without guidance.

Our goal: validate clarity, discoverability, and ease-of-use across the redesigned layout.

  • Can you post an update to Twitter or Facebook?

Contextual Simplicity

We minimized clutter by showing only what matters right now:

  • Just went live? Show chat, title, and stream health.
  • Getting followers? Highlight engagement stats.
  • Stream ending? Show clip/export tools.

Lessons

  • Less interface = more confidence
    New creators were overwhelmed by too many tools. Reducing what we showed built clarity and momentum.
  • Good defaults are invisible help
    A Twitch-guided layout helped creators skip configuration and focus on streaming.
  • Customization is good. Guided customization is better.
    People don‚Äôt want to build a dashboard, they want to go live.

Conclusion

The Stream Manager redesign wasn‚Äôt about packing in more controls. It was about getting out of the way.

By designing around actions, not apps, and guidance, not GUIs, we helped creators succeed faster, with fewer buttons, fewer doubts, and more joy.
